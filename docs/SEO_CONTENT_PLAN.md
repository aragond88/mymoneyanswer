# MyMoneyAnswer SEO Content Plan

This document turns the site goal into an execution plan for organic search growth.

The goal is not to create many pages quickly. The goal is to build useful topic clusters around the main MyMoneyAnswer promise: helping beginners decide their next best money move in 5 minutes.

## Strategy

MyMoneyAnswer should rank by becoming a practical decision hub, not a generic personal finance blog.

The strongest pages should combine:

- A clear beginner question.
- A useful tool, calculator, checklist, comparison, or decision framework.
- United States and Canada context when it helps the reader.
- Simple English.
- Internal links to the next useful step.
- Educational disclaimers for investing, debt, taxes, affiliate products, or financial decisions.

## Google Trends Monitoring

Google Trends should influence publishing priority, but it should not replace the site's core strategy.

Use this rule:

- Evergreen pages build the foundation.
- Trending topics decide timing.
- Avoid chasing trends that do not fit the beginner money decision tool promise.

### Monthly Trend Review

Once per month, review Google Trends for:

- United States, past 12 months.
- Canada, past 12 months.
- United States, past 30 days.
- Canada, past 30 days.
- Personal finance and business categories when relevant.

Compare related queries for:

- Emergency fund.
- Debt payoff.
- Credit card debt.
- High-yield savings account.
- High-interest savings account Canada.
- Roth Individual Retirement Account.
- 401(k) retirement plan.
- Tax-Free Savings Account.
- Registered Retirement Savings Plan.
- Index funds.
- Compound interest calculator.
- Mortgage rates.
- Taxes.
- Student loans.

### Trend Topics That Fit MyMoneyAnswer

Pursue trends when they connect to a practical beginner decision:

- Mortgage rates: create educational pages about saving, debt, housing affordability, and emergency planning rather than mortgage rate news.
- Taxes: explain account basics, tax season checklists, and beginner account tradeoffs with clear disclaimers.
- High-yield savings accounts: connect to emergency fund storage and account comparison basics.
- High-interest savings accounts in Canada: connect to emergency fund storage and Wealthsimple only when relevant and disclosed.
- Credit card debt: connect to payoff plans, minimum payments, avalanche, and snowball.
- Student loans: connect to debt prioritization and cash flow planning.
- Retirement contribution limits: create update-friendly account pages, but verify official sources before publishing.
- Artificial intelligence finance tools: only cover if the page helps beginners evaluate budgeting, tracking, or planning tools safely.
- Coast Financial Independence Retire Early calculators: only pursue after the core calculators and beginner investing pages are stronger.

### Trend Topics To Avoid For Now

Avoid topics that are too speculative, news-driven, or misaligned with the site promise:

- Cryptocurrency price predictions.
- Stock picks.
- Day trading.
- Meme stocks.
- Guaranteed income opportunities.
- Precious metals hype.
- Political tax speculation without official rules.
- Product rankings created mainly for affiliate commissions.

### Seasonal Content Calendar

Use seasonal search behavior to prioritize updates:

- January to April: taxes, retirement account contributions, account basics, savings goals.
- April to June: debt payoff, emergency fund rebuilding, mid-year money review.
- July to September: student loans, back-to-school budgeting, credit card debt, savings plans.
- October to December: year-end money checklist, tax-loss harvesting basics, contribution deadlines, holiday debt prevention.

### Decision Rule For New Trend Pages

Before creating a trend-based page, confirm:

- The topic has a real beginner question.
- The page can remain useful after the trend fades.
- The page can link to at least one MyMoneyAnswer tool.
- The content does not require daily updates.
- The page can cite reliable or official sources when rules, rates, limits, or product details are involved.
- The page does not make predictions, guarantees, or personalized recommendations.

## Page Template Requirements

Every new indexable article should include:

- Unique title.
- Unique meta description.
- Canonical URL.
- Open Graph tags.
- One clear H1.
- Helpful introduction that directly answers the search intent.
- Educational disclaimer near the top when discussing investing, debt, taxes, affiliate products, or financial decisions.
- Clear H2 sections.
- Internal links to related tools and articles.
- Frequently asked questions when useful.
- Schema.org structured data when relevant.
- Last updated date.
- Sources section for pages involving changing rules, account limits, tax treatment, product details, or investment assumptions.

## Internal Linking Rules

Each new article should link back to at least one tool:

- Main quiz: `./index.html`
- Investment calculator: `./calculator.html`
- Emergency fund calculator: `./emergency-fund-calculator.html`
- Debt payoff calculator: `./debt-payoff-calculator.html`

Each tool page should link to the strongest related article in its cluster.

The blog hub should group articles by topic:

- Start Here
- Emergency Fund
- Debt Payoff
- Beginner Investing
- United States Accounts
- Canada Accounts
- Product Reviews

## Monetization Rules

Do not add Google Ads until the content foundation is stronger and the user explicitly approves it.

Do not add new affiliate programs yet.

Wealthsimple may be used as the near-term Canada affiliate candidate only when:

- The page is relevant to Canadian users.
- The page remains useful without the affiliate link.
- The affiliate disclosure appears before or near the link.
- The product discussion is neutral.
- Product details are reviewed before publishing.

## Quality Bar Before Publishing Each Page

Before a new page goes live:

- Check grammar and readability.
- Check all links are relative where internal.
- Check disclaimers are present.
- Check no guaranteed outcome claims are made.
- Check no personalized advice claims are made.
- Check the page is not thin or duplicated.
- Check sources for current account rules, tax rules, fees, product details, or contribution limits.
- Update `sitemap.xml`.
- Add internal links from related existing pages.
